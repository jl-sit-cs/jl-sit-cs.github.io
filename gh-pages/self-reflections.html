About Our Project

Our Mission

Our mission is to make species identification more accessible and engaging by integrating citizen science tools like iNaturalist, gamification, and educational features. We aim to foster a culture of collaborative learning and biodiversity recording, supported by expert validation to ensure scientific accuracy.

Problem Statement

How might we encourage citizens and visitors to collect more sighting data to improve their understanding and educate about their characteristics while maintaining efficient scientific records?

What I'm Proud Of

I'm proud of taking the initiative to design our presentation materials using Canva, which helped us create professional and visually appealing slides for the pitch videos. Looking back, I wish I had been more involved in the early research phase. While I focused on design and technical work, I could have contributed more to stakeholder engagement and user research. Partner Interactions & Stakeholder Engagement

Key Partnership

Glasgow Botanic Gardens

Our primary stakeholder relationship was with Chrissy Sanachan from the Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This partnership was crucial in understanding the real-world challenges and requirements for species identification and data collection.

Glasgow, Scotland

Interaction Overview

Initial Requirements Gathering

Understanding current workflows and pain points

Prototype Feedback Sessions

Iterative design validation and refinement

Ongoing Communication

Regular updates and collaborative decision-making

Team Reflections on Partner Interactions

What Worked Well

  • Clear Communication: Chrissy provided detailed insights into current species recording processes and challenges
  • Domain Expertise: Her knowledge of botanical gardens operations helped us understand real-world constraints
  • Collaborative Approach: Open to our ideas while providing practical feedback on feasibility
  • Timely Feedback: Responsive to our prototypes and provided constructive criticism

Challenges Faced

  • Time Constraints: Limited availability for extended feedback sessions due to busy schedules
  • Technical Gap: Bridging the gap between technical possibilities and practical implementation
  • Scope Management: Balancing ambitious ideas with realistic project timelines
  • Remote Coordination: Managing communication and feedback collection across different locations

Key Learnings from Partner Engagement

Active Listening

Understanding stakeholder needs requires deep listening and asking the right questions to uncover underlying challenges and requirements.

Iterative Collaboration

Regular feedback loops with stakeholders are essential for creating solutions that truly meet their needs and can be successfully implemented.

Balancing Perspectives

Successful partnerships require balancing innovative ideas with practical constraints and existing organizational workflows.

Impact on Project Development

Design Decisions Influenced

  • • Focus on iNaturalist integration based on existing platform familiarity
  • • Emphasis on user-friendly interfaces for citizen scientists
  • • Incorporation of expert validation workflows
  • • Consideration of offline functionality for field work

Project Scope Refinement

  • • Prioritized features based on real-world usage patterns
  • • Adjusted technical complexity to match implementation capacity
  • • Focused on sustainable, long-term solutions
  • • Aligned project goals with organizational objectives
