User-Centric Problem Statement

"How might we encourage botanic friends and visitors to collect more sighting data to improve their understanding and educate about their characteristics while maintaining efficient scientific records?"

User Pain Points

  • • Manual data entry is time-consuming and error-prone
  • • Limited engagement with species identification
  • • Lack of educational value in current processes
  • • Disconnected workflows between experts and citizens

Stakeholder Needs

  • • Efficient data collection and validation
  • • Increased community participation
  • • Maintained scientific accuracy
  • • Year-round engagement opportunities

Success Metrics

  • • Reduced manual data entry time
  • • Increased citizen scientist participation
  • • Improved data quality and accuracy
  • • Enhanced educational outcomes

Solution Proposed

iNaturalist-Enhanced Citizen Science Platform for Botanical Gardens

A comprehensive solution that integrates iNaturalist's proven AI identification technology with custom workflows designed specifically for botanical gardens, featuring gamification elements to encourage citizen participation while maintaining expert validation processes for scientific accuracy.

Core Technology

  • • iNaturalist API integration
  • • AI-powered species identification
  • • Real-time confidence scoring
  • • Community validation system

User Experience

  • • Intuitive mobile-first interface
  • • Gamification and achievement systems
  • • Educational content integration
  • • Progress tracking and leaderboards

Expert Workflows

  • • Streamlined validation dashboard
  • • Automated data quality checks
  • • Expert review and approval system
  • • Integration with garden databases

Solution Justification

Why Our iNaturalist-Enhanced Solution is the Optimal Choice

Our solution choice is based on comprehensive analysis of stakeholder needs, technical feasibility, user adoption potential, and implementation constraints. The decision to enhance iNaturalist rather than build from scratch is supported by evidence from multiple validation sources.

Evidence-Based Rationale

Stakeholder Validation

Chrissy from Glasgow Botanic Gardens confirmed:

  • • Current manual processes are time-consuming and error-prone
  • • Need for increased community engagement in species recording
  • • Importance of maintaining scientific accuracy and expert oversight
  • • Preference for leveraging existing proven platforms

Technical Feasibility Analysis

iNaturalist integration offers:

  • • Proven AI accuracy with 95%+ identification success rate
  • • Robust API with comprehensive documentation
  • • Established infrastructure handling millions of observations
  • • Active community of 2M+ users for validation

User Adoption Evidence

Research shows:

  • • 70% faster user onboarding with familiar interfaces
  • • Higher engagement rates with gamification elements
  • • Reduced learning curve for citizen scientists
  • • Existing trust and credibility in scientific community

Stakeholder Alignment & Validation

Glasgow Botanic Gardens Approval

  • • Confirmed solution addresses core pain points
  • • Validated integration with existing workflows
  • • Approved gamification approach for engagement
  • • Endorsed expert validation processes

User Community Validation

  • • Positive feedback on prototype testing
  • • Confirmed intuitive user experience

Team Dynamics & Collaboration

Our team of 6 members brought diverse perspectives to the species identification challenge. Each member contributed their unique strengths - from research and technical skills to design and communication abilities. We operated with a collaborative approach where everyone complemented one another's strengths, ensuring that individual expertise in different areas converged into a cohesive and well-rounded solution. Regular team meetings and open communication allowed us to leverage each member's talents while maintaining alignment on project goals.

Pre-existing Solutions Analysis

Current Market Solutions

iNaturalist

Global citizen science platform with AI identification

✓ Large user base, proven AI, community validation

✗ Not tailored for botanical gardens, limited expert workflows

PlantNet

Plant identification app with collaborative database

✓ Plant-focused, good accuracy

✗ Limited gamification, no institutional integration

Manual Spreadsheets

Traditional data collection methods

✓ Familiar, customizable

✗ Time-consuming, error-prone, not engaging

Our Solution Advantage

Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

  • • Lack of institutional integration - No seamless workflow for botanical gardens
  • • Limited engagement - Missing gamification and educational elements
  • • Expert validation gaps - No streamlined expert review processes
  • • Data silos - Disconnected from existing garden management systems

Our Unique Approach

Combining iNaturalist's proven technology with custom workflows for botanical gardens, gamification for engagement, and streamlined expert validation creates a comprehensive solution addressing all identified gaps.
