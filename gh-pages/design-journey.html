1

Week 1: Research & Discovery

Understanding the problem space and stakeholder needs

Key Activities

  • • Stakeholder interview with Chrissy Sanachan from Glasgow Botanic Gardens
  • • Analysis of current species identification and data collection workflows
  • • Research into iNaturalist platform capabilities and citizen science best practices
  • • Initial problem definition and opportunity identification

Key Insights

Main Challenge: Manual data entry processes are time-consuming and prone to errors, limiting the scale and consistency of species monitoring.

Opportunity: Leveraging citizen science and AI-powered identification tools could significantly enhance data collection while engaging the public in biodiversity conservation.

Exploring solutions and developing testable concepts

  • • Prototype development for iNaturalist integration concepts
  • • Initial testing and feedback collection

Workflow for expert review and validation of citizen science contributions

3

Week 3: Solution Development

Refining and validating the final solution

Refinement Process

  • • Integration of feedback from stakeholder and user testing
  • • Development of implementation roadmap and technical specifications
  • • Creation of training materials and user guides
  • • Final validation testing and impact assessment

Final Solution

A comprehensive citizen science platform integrating iNaturalist's AI capabilities with expert validation workflows, designed to:

  • • Reduce manual data entry by 70%
  • • Increase citizen participation by 300%
  • • Maintain scientific accuracy through expert review
  • • Enable year-round biodiversity monitoring

Solution Verification

Stakeholder Validation

Regular feedback sessions with Glasgow Botanic Gardens staff and citizen science communities

Performance Metrics

Quantitative analysis of efficiency improvements and accuracy measurements

Technical Validation

Testing of iNaturalist API integration and workflow automation capabilities

Key Learnings & Insights

What Worked Well

  • • Early stakeholder engagement provided clear direction
  • • Iterative prototyping allowed rapid concept validation
  • • Focus on user experience improved adoption potential
  • • Integration approach balanced automation with expert oversight

Areas for Improvement

  • • More extensive user testing with diverse citizen scientists
  • • Earlier technical feasibility assessment
  • • Broader stakeholder engagement across the ecosystem
  • • More detailed implementation timeline planning
