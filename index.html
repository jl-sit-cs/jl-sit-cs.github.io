Project Timeline

22 July 2025

Requirement Gathering

Initial requirement gathering with our stakeholder (Chrissy Sanachan) to understand the specific needs and objectives for the species identification project at Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This actively involves researching on the current workflow of application such as iNaturalist and other similar platforms. Additionally, gathering key insights from the stakeholder to inform the design and development process.

22 July - 25 July 2025

First Pitch Video

The video serves as an introduction to the project, outlining the problem statement, objectives, and initial research findings. It includes a brief overview of the current workflow of species identification and highlights the key challenges faced by users in the existing systems. Additionally, crafting a prototype to understand the user journey and identify areas for improvement. Therefore, allowing the team to visualize the the number of steps for the user to identify the species.

26 July - 1 August 2025

Second Pitch Video

The second pitch video builds upon the initial findings and presents a refined prototype based on user feedback and further research. It actively showcases the proposed solution, highlighting the key features and functionalities that address the identified challenges in species identification. The video serves as a platform to communicate the project's progress and gather additional feedback from stakeholders.

1 August - 5 August 2025

Poster Report

Crafting a summarized poster which encapsulates the project idea, problem statement and proposed solutions, which actively highlights the key findings from the research and design process. The poster serves as a visual representation of the project progress and is designed to effectively communicate the project's objectives, methodologies, and outcomes to a wider audience.

1 August - 8 August 2025

Website Documentation

Developing a comprehensive website documentation which outlines the project description, objectives, and methodologies. The website serves as a central hub for all project-related information, including the research findings, design process, and final outcomes. It is designed to be user-friendly and accessible, allowing stakeholders and the public to easily navigate through the project's details and understand its impact.
