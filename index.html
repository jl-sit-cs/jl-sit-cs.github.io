🎯 Problem Statement & Challenge

⚠️ Current Challenges

  • 🔴 Manual species recording and data entry processes
  • ⏰ Time-consuming and error-prone workflows
  • 👥 Limited citizen participation in data collection
  • 📊 Inconsistent year-round monitoring

💡 Project Goal

"How might we encourage citizens and visitors to collect more sighting data to improve their understanding and educate about species characteristics while maintaining efficient scientific records?"

🎯 Goal: Streamline species identification and data collection at Glasgow Botanic Gardens through citizen science and AI-powered tools.

🌿 Project Overview & Vision

🎯 Our Mission

This project aims to enhance species recording by reducing manual data entry and leveraging citizen science tools like iNaturalist. The system encourages collaborative recording throughout the year while preserving expert review where needed.

🌱 Key Focus Areas

  • • Streamline data collection workflows
  • • Integrate citizen science participation
  • • Maintain scientific accuracy and validation

📈 Expected Impact

Enable continuous year-round data collection while maintaining expert validation and improving biodiversity monitoring accuracy at Glasgow Botanic Gardens.

🚀 Project Benefits

  • • Reduced manual workload for staff
  • • Increased community engagement
  • • Enhanced data quality and coverage

🔬 Our Methodology & Approach

👥 User-Centered Design

Our team approached this challenge through a comprehensive three-week design process, combining user research, stakeholder engagement, and iterative prototyping to develop a citizen science solution.

🔍 Research Methods

  • • Stakeholder interviews and analysis
  • • Workflow observation and mapping
  • • User journey documentation

🔄 Iterative Development

Through rapid prototyping and continuous feedback loops, we refined our solutions to ensure they meet both user needs and technical requirements.

⚡ Design Process

  • • Rapid prototyping and testing
  • • Continuous stakeholder feedback
  • • Iterative solution refinement
